## What Is Periodontics?

**Sub-title: Focused care for your gums and supporting structures**

Periodontics is a dental specialty dedicated to the health of your gums and the structures that support your teeth. This field addresses conditions like gingivitis and periodontitis, which, if left untreated, can lead to tooth loss and other health complications. Through various treatments, periodontics aims to restore and maintain the integrity of your oral tissues.

---

## Who Needs Periodontal Treatment?

**Sub-title: Recognizing the signs and risk factors**

You may benefit from periodontal care if you:

- Experience bleeding or swollen gums  
- Notice persistent bad breath  
- Have loose or shifting teeth  
- See gum recession or pockets forming between teeth and gums  
- Have health conditions like diabetes that increase gum disease risk

Early intervention is key. Regular check-ups can catch issues before they escalate, ensuring better outcomes and preserving your natural teeth.

## Common Periodontal Treatments

**Sub-title: Effective solutions for gum health**

Our periodontal services include:

- **Scaling and Root Planing** – A deep-cleaning method to remove plaque and tartar beneath the gum line.  
- **Laser Therapy** – Minimally invasive treatment to reduce bacteria and promote healing.  
- **Gum Grafting** – Restores receding gums and protects tooth roots.  
- **Pocket Reduction Surgery** – Reduces the depth of gum pockets to prevent bacterial accumulation.  
- **Dental Implants** – Replace missing teeth and maintain jawbone integrity.

Each treatment is selected based on your specific condition and comfort.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Periodontics

**Sub-title: Understanding periodontal care**

### What causes gum disease?  
Gum disease is primarily caused by plaque buildup, leading to inflammation and infection of gum tissues.

### Is periodontal treatment painful?  
Most treatments are performed under local anesthesia, minimizing discomfort during procedures.

### How can I prevent gum disease?  
Regular brushing, flossing, and dental check-ups are key to preventing gum disease.
